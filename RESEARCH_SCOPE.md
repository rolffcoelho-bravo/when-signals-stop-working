# Research Scope and Standard of Evidence

## Objective

Determine whether RSI and Bollinger Band information contributes stable, benchmark-relative predictive and economic value for the next four-hour SOL/USDT return, and define the governance conditions under which an established contribution may be classified as active, reduced, or suspended.

## Candidate specifications

- RSI as a momentum-state variable family;
- Bollinger Bands as a volatility-relative price-location variable family;
- a secondary combined specification testing complementarity between the two information families.

## Evidence gates

### 1. Descriptive event evidence

Conventional threshold events are documented without implying causality or persistent forecasting value.

### 2. Incremental predictive evidence

Each candidate is compared with the same non-indicator benchmark on unseen chronological observations.

### 3. Incremental economic evidence

Any predictive contribution is evaluated after the declared cost assumption and with dependence-aware uncertainty intervals.

### 4. Persistence evidence

An established contribution is evaluated across filtered market states and through sequential deterioration monitoring.

## Status taxonomy

- `NOT_ESTABLISHED` - the historical establishment requirement was not met;
- `ACTIVE` - established value remains positive under current evidence and monitoring rules;
- `REDUCED` - historical value exists, but current evidence is uncertain, regime-concentrated, or deteriorating;
- `SUSPENDED` - established value has crossed the structural-deterioration and recent-performance gates.

## Standard of evidence

A candidate is not promoted because it performs favourably in isolated periods, appears compelling in a chart, or outperforms after unrestricted parameter search. Promotion requires predeclared assumptions, common-benchmark comparison, chronological validation, complete fold reporting, and explicit model boundaries.

## Non-objectives

Version 1 does not seek to identify optimal trading parameters, simulate production execution, estimate market capacity, or make a universal claim regarding technical indicators. These questions require separate research designs and are addressed only in later methodological phases.
