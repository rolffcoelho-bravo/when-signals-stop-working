# Version 3 Multi-Asset Causal Feature and Spectral Engine

## Purpose

Gate V3-2 converts the canonical market-data layer into a forward-measurable description of market stress and dependence structure. It does not classify panic, select RSI or Bollinger specifications, forecast returns, or authorize decisions.

The output is the governed market-structure information set used by Gate V3-3:

\[
Z_t = \left(\text{causal stress features},\;\text{dependence structure},\;\text{spectral structure}\right)_t.
\]

## Fixed-panel rule

Every run requires an explicit ordered list of `asset@venue` members. Panel membership is not inferred from future availability or performance. An absent registered member stops execution. A member with insufficient observations remains visible through `INSUFFICIENT_PANEL_COVERAGE`; the engine does not silently remove it.

## Causal series features

For every registered asset and venue, the engine generates information measurable through timestamp `t`, including:

- one-period log return;
- drawdown and drawdown velocity;
- downside-return acceleration;
- downside semivariance;
- realised volatility at registered windows;
- volume change and abnormal-volume score;
- bid-ask-spread level and stress score where available;
- order-book-depth change and imbalance where available;
- funding-rate stress and open-interest change where available;
- liquidation intensity and net liquidation pressure where available;
- net exchange flow and cross-venue dispersion where available.

No centred window, future fill, retrospective smoothing, or target-derived transformation is used.

## Dependence estimators

The registered estimator is selected in configuration before evaluation. Supported estimators are:

1. sample covariance and correlation;
2. exponentially weighted covariance and correlation;
3. diagonal-shrinkage covariance and correlation.

Every estimator operates on complete observations from the fixed panel inside the trailing registered window. Pairwise deletion and silent panel shrinkage are prohibited.

## Spectral measures

For correlation matrix \(R_t\) with ordered eigenvalues
\(\lambda_{1,t} \ge \dots \ge \lambda_{N,t}\), the engine reports:

- dominant eigenvalue and dominant-eigenvalue share;
- normalized eigenvalue gap;
- participation ratio and effective dimension;
- normalized spectral entropy;
- first-eigenvector concentration;
- dominant-share change;
- sign-invariant first-eigenvector stability;
- average pairwise correlation and correlation dispersion;
- threshold-network density and degree concentration;
- complete eigenvalue spectrum.

The dominant-eigenvalue share is

\[
\frac{\lambda_{1,t}}{\sum_{j=1}^{N}\lambda_{j,t}}.
\]

The participation ratio is

\[
\frac{\left(\sum_j \lambda_{j,t}\right)^2}
{\sum_j \lambda_{j,t}^2}.
\]

A high dominant share and low effective dimension indicate stronger common-mode concentration, but neither quantity is labelled as panic by this gate.

## Eligibility controls

Each timestamp and window receives one of the following states:

```text
ELIGIBLE
INSUFFICIENT_PANEL_COVERAGE
INELIGIBLE_DEPENDENCE_WINDOW
```

Eligibility requires:

- the complete registered trailing window;
- minimum total panel coverage;
- minimum complete-case observations;
- finite observations for all registered members;
- positive variance for every panel member.

Missing critical evidence fails closed.

## Sensitivity evidence

All registered windows remain in the output. The diagnostics summarize eligibility and spectral behaviour by window and report cross-window dominant-share correlation. They explicitly record `selection_performed: false`.

Registered panel-composition challengers may be compared with `compare_registered_panels`. The function produces descriptive evidence for every predeclared panel and does not choose a favourable panel.

## Reproducible outputs

The runner writes:

```text
spectral_market_structure.csv
causal_series_features.csv
spectral_manifest.json
spectral_diagnostics.json
canonical_validation_report.json
```

The manifest binds the input data, configuration, fixed panel, estimator and generated output through deterministic hashes.

## Execution

```powershell
.\RUN_V3_G2_SPECTRAL_ENGINE.ps1 `
  -Config "configs/v3_spectral_example.json" `
  -OutputDirectory "outputs/v3/spectral"
```

The example configuration is a template. Its panel members must exist in the canonical input generated by Gate V3-1.

## Model boundaries

Gate V3-2 does not establish that a panic-consistent regime exists. It does not establish predictive or economic value for RSI, Bollinger Bands or any other signal. It does not select a trading strategy. Its sole role is to create a leakage-controlled, auditable market-structure layer for subsequent regime modelling and conditional signal validation.
