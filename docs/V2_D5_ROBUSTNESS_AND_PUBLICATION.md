# Version 2 D5 Robustness and Publication Evidence

## Research function

D5 closes the Version 2 primary-case evidence chain. It does not estimate a new
model. It evaluates whether the already-frozen D4 result is concentrated,
influence-sensitive, chronologically fragile, state-dependent, or dependent on
unstable development selections.

## Diagnostic layers

### Dependence and cost sensitivity

The registered moving-block lengths and transaction-cost assumptions are
reported together. A favourable mean is not treated as established value when
the relevant lower confidence bound crosses zero.

### Chronological concentration

The evidence is assessed by locked subperiod, calendar month, and
leave-one-month-out exclusion. These diagnostics identify whether a small
calendar segment drives the average result.

### Influence sensitivity

Joint tail trimming tests whether a small number of extreme predictive or
economic contributions determine the sign of the result. Trimming is a
diagnostic only and does not replace the frozen confirmatory statistic.

### State and confidence stratification

The frozen predictions are summarized by the existing filtered state label and
by candidate-confidence strata. These are descriptive outputs. In particular,
the small stress-state sample cannot support a causal or confirmatory panic
claim.

### Development component stability

The D2B outer-development selections are summarized by model family,
estimation window, regime conditioning, calibration, threshold, and signal
parameterization. No alternative pipeline is executed on the locked period.

## Interpretation control

D5 may identify favourable diagnostics, but it cannot:

- change `NO_INCREMENTAL_EVIDENCE`;
- promote the primary case;
- authorize deployment or monitoring;
- reopen RSI;
- retune Bollinger;
- incorporate V2.1 into the Version 2 verdict.

## External replication

The predeclared external-replication layer is not triggered as a route to
establishment because the primary confirmatory gate failed. External markets
may be used later for independent negative-result replication or for the
separately frozen V2.1 panic-state study, but they cannot rescue Version 2.
